Aptible Conveyor

Head of Design

Began by working as the second designer on Aptible Comply, a governance, risk, and compliance platform. Crossed teams to be the sole designer working on what was then a side product. That product, Rooms, ended up doing so well that we spun out of Aptible into our own company, Conveyor, with me as the first designer.

Now, Conveyor is working on making vendor security reviews fast, frictionless, and accurate by facilitating connections between B2B SaaS companies.

What I did

As a manager and leader:

  • In collaboration with a Senior PM, ran a 2-week “vision sprint” to crystallize our company vision and strategy through customer research and discovery. This project lead to increased insights, alignment, and excitement among the broader team through our results and storytelling.
  • Redesigned our hiring process to optimize the candidate experience while gathering the data we need to make a high-confidence decision in a structured and objective way.
  • Coached and leveled up 2 reports.
  • Facilitated the creation of our team’s first set of design principles.
  • Provided thoughtful and actionable feedback to help my teammates do their best work and encouraged psychological safety on the team. The design team consistently saw high scores on company pulse surveys and my product squad had some of the highest performance and retention in the company.
  • Advocated to create the best experience for those impacted by layoffs in May of 2021, and created a supportive space for remaining team members.

As a designer:

  • Consistently shipped high-impact features that solidified customer relationships with our flagship customers and led to us being able to upsell Rooms from a free product into substantial recurring revenue.
  • Redesigned our sign-up/log in flow to incorporate passwordless login and reduce customer confusion, leading to a nearly 100% reduction in sign-up-related support tickets and accidental sign-ups.
  • Designed multiple 0 → 1 products for Conveyor. Our Rooms product led to the acquisition of flagship customers like Datadog, PagerDuty, and dbt. Our vendor network product resulted in rapidly growing user conversion and increased participation and engagement in customer research calls.
  • Contributed to our design system, Arrow, including upgrading and refactoring components to use Figma interactive variants, making it easier for our team of designers to leverage.
  • Optimized my own personal processes for facilitating collaboration, gathering feedback and user research insights, and creating high ROI prototypes and designs.
  • At Aptible, shipped experiences that made our customers excited they could finally use a GRC product that didn’t suck.

What I learned

  • It’s never too early to get feedback. Just make sure you communicate in a way that solicits the right kind of feedback for the stage of the project.
  • Leveling up as a designer is as much about knowing what would have a low ROI to do as it is knowing the best way to do something.
  • Take the time to structure your components. You'll thank you later.
  • Most conflict comes from mismatched expectations.
  • Doing the right thing may be hard in the moment but the best case scenario is that you’ll be glad in hindsight. Worst case scenario you’ll be neutral. Either way, it’s worth doing.
